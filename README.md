## Welcome

You're taking part in a technical exercise as part of your interview with us at TSB. The goal is simple: build something, think through it, and come ready to walk us through what you did and why.

There's no single right answer here. We're more interested in how you approach a problem than whether everything is perfect.

## The problem

Bank reconciliation is the process of matching bank transactions against accounting records, things like invoices and bills, to make sure the books add up.

Sometimes there's a single record that matches a transaction exactly. Sometimes it's a combination of records that together add up to the right amount. Right now, users have to figure that out themselves. There's probably a smarter way to handle it.

## What's here

If you run the project, you'll find a single screen showing a list of accounting records and a transaction total. That's your starting point.

## What we'd like you to do

There are three tasks in total. Please attempt all of them.

**Task 1 and 2 involve writing code. Task 3 does not.**

---

### Task 1
When a user selects an accounting record, subtract its value from the remaining total shown at the top.

---

### Task 2
When the screen first loads, if a single accounting record matches the total exactly, select it automatically.

Don't overthink the scope, but don't hold back either. How you structure your solution, what patterns you reach for, and the decisions you make along the way are all fair game for conversation.

If you run out of time or hit a wall on Tasks 1 or 2, that's fine. We'd rather talk through an honest attempt than a rushed one.

---

### Task 3 — Thought experiment (written response, no code)

How would you handle automatically selecting a *combination* of accounting records that sum to the transaction total?

This is a design and thinking exercise. We want you to write down your approach: how you'd solve the problem, what you'd consider, any tradeoffs or edge cases worth calling out. There's no need to write any code for this one.

Come ready to walk us through your thinking during the interview.
